Figure 25b.1. Metabolism of Se. Note that inorganic Se can be incorporated into SeCys-containing selenoproteins by way of the obligate intermediate selenide (H2Se); while Se from SeMet can also be incorporated into the SeCys-containing proteins by way of catabolism to H2Se, and into other proteins directly by competing with methionine (Met) in protein synthesis.
